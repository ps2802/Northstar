# Gridlock Calibration Audit

Scope: evaluate Northstar's current onboarding and task generation quality for `https://joingridlock.com/`.

## Current Northstar Output

### Company understanding
- Company summary: Gridlock appears to be a Formula 1 prediction game built for fans who care about qualifying, grid outcomes, and outperforming consensus. The positioning leans into skill, competition, and season-long participation rather than a generic casual sports game.
- ICP: Formula 1 fans who follow qualifying closely and want a more skill-driven prediction game than casual pick-em products.
- Opportunities:
  - The core hook is clear, but the site should do more to explain the season loop, retention mechanics, and why this game is more skill-based than generic prediction products.
  - The product can likely benefit from social and launch-style content that rides qualifying weekends, race weekends, and season moments.
  - There is little visible search surface beyond the homepage, so discoverability and acquisition content may be underbuilt.

### Generated backlog
- Sharpen the homepage story for serious F1 fans joining Gridlock
- Create an X post set for qualifying-day engagement
- Plan a race-weekend content cluster for Gridlock
- Draft a blog brief on why F1 predictions should reward skill over consensus
- Scan adjacent F1 fantasy and prediction products

### Artifact direction
- Blog brief: `Draft a blog brief on why F1 predictions should reward skill over consensus`
- The direction is founder-usable because it ties the product philosophy to a clear audience and channel strategy.

## What Northstar gets right

- It correctly identifies Gridlock as a consumer sports product, not generic SaaS.
- It understands the F1-specific cadence around qualifying and race weekends.
- The ICP is directionally right: serious F1 fans who care about the season and the competitive layer.
- The first backlog item is concrete and directly tied to conversion.
- The opportunity list is useful for a founder because it points to season loop, retention, and acquisition channels.
- The blog brief is aligned with the brand point of view instead of being a random SEO assignment.

## Where it sounds generic or wrong

- The `keyword cluster` style task is still a little generic in wording, even though the theme is now sports-specific.
- The `scan adjacent F1 fantasy and prediction products` task is useful, but it still reads like a standard competitor review template.
- The `X post set` task is relevant, but its value is implied more than made explicit in a business-outcome sense.
- The `homepage story` task is good, but it could be more direct about signup conversion and first-session activation.

## Where it confuses the business model

- Northstar no longer confuses Gridlock with B2B, which is good.
- The remaining risk is subtler: it can still treat the product like a generic prediction game instead of a season-based sports experience with recurring event-driven engagement.
- The current output does not yet deeply distinguish acquisition, activation, and retention mechanics for a consumer sports game.
- The product will need more explicit attention to how users come back during the F1 calendar, not just how they discover the site.

## Where task generation is too vague

- `Plan a race-weekend content cluster for Gridlock` is directionally good, but it should specify what the cluster is meant to drive: signup, repeat play, or social amplification.
- `Scan adjacent F1 fantasy and prediction products` should specify the lens: onboarding, reward loop, social virality, or retention.
- `Create an X post set for qualifying-day engagement` should say whether the goal is awareness, activation, or reactivation.
- The generated backlog is useful, but the execution intent on some tasks is still implicit rather than explicit.

## What should be fixed before live demos

1. Make the business outcome explicit in each task title or rationale, especially for social and content tasks.
2. Keep the consumer-sports tone, but make the season loop and retention story more visible in the summary.
3. Tighten the wording on competitor and content tasks so they feel like founder work, not template marketing work.
4. Preserve the strong F1 framing, because that is already materially better than the earlier generic output.
5. Use a slightly sharper distinction between awareness work and conversion work when ordering the backlog.

## Priority order

1. Homepage story for serious F1 fans
2. X post set for qualifying-day engagement
3. Race-weekend content cluster
4. Blog brief on skill over consensus
5. Adjacent-product scan
