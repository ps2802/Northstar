# Northstar Self Audit

Scope: dogfood Northstar on Northstar using the current local repo and local API output.

## Before / After

### Before fix

- Company summary: Northstar was misread as a GitHub-first agentic QA product.
- ICP: Product, growth, and engineering teams buying overnight QA audits.
- Opportunities: repo-first QA positioning, overnight delivery, and GitHub-native audit proof.
- Backlog: QA-style homepage narrative, audit keywords, overnight QA blog brief, generic founder posts, QA competitor scan.

### After fix

- Company summary: Northstar now reads as a Kanban-first operating system for non-technical founders.
- ICP: Non-technical founders and lean startup operators who need a visible system to prioritize, supervise, and approve growth work.
- Opportunities: board-led trust, dogfood proof, and founder-facing category language.
- Backlog: founder-OS homepage narrative, board-led growth keyword map, founder-OS blog brief, proof-led founder signal set, founder-tool competitor framing.

## What Northstar generated for itself

### Company understanding

- Company summary: Northstar appears to be a Kanban-first operating system for non-technical founders. The core promise is that a founder can connect a website, generate a prioritized growth backlog, and manage execution and approvals from one board instead of juggling disconnected marketing work.
- ICP: Non-technical founders and lean startup operators who need a visible system to prioritize, supervise, and approve growth work without hiring a full marketing team.
- Opportunities:
  - The product promise is strong, but the first-time story should explain faster why a founder should trust a board-led operating system over a chat-first assistant.
  - The repo shows execution credibility, but Northstar still needs sharper public proof that the board creates prioritized, founder-usable growth work rather than generic task lists.
  - The best growth angle is likely dogfooding and operator education, but that point of view needs clearer landing-page and content framing.

### Generated backlog

- Clarify why Northstar is a founder operating system, not just another chatbot
- Map the search language around founder operating systems, marketing boards, and execution visibility
- Draft a blog brief on why non-technical founders need a board, not another chat thread
- Review how adjacent founder tools frame prioritization, visibility, and approvals
- Create a founder-proof signal set from Northstar's own dogfood results

### Artifact

- Blog brief: `Draft a blog brief on why non-technical founders need a board, not another chat thread`
- Direction: explain the messy before-state, make the board-led operating model feel inevitable, and tie the argument back to founder control and approvals.

## What looked strong

- Northstar now understands its own product identity correctly.
- ICP follows the product thesis instead of the repo implementation details.
- The backlog now reads like work a founder would actually use to explain and sell Northstar.
- The top task is the right one: sharpen the buyer-facing founder-OS thesis first.
- Task rationale is clearer because each task now states the business outcome it supports.
- The approval flow still works cleanly after the content and classification changes.

## What looked weak or generic

- The company summary is correct, but it still leans on the repo surface more than a polished product site would.
- The keyword and competitor tasks are much better, but they could still drift into generic productivity language if the category framing weakens.
- The proof-led founder signal set is better than a generic social task, but it still depends on strong dogfood evidence to feel concrete.
- The blog brief is directionally right, but the artifact template is still somewhat generic in structure.

## Where it confuses the business model

- The main business-model confusion is fixed.
- The remaining risk is category drift: Northstar could still sound like a productivity tool if the founder-OS framing is not kept explicit.
- The repo should stay proof of execution, not the category label.

## Where task generation is still too vague

- The founder-proof signal set needs strong underlying examples to avoid sounding like abstract founder content.
- The competitor scan should stay anchored on board-first operating systems and chat-first assistants, not generic project tools.
- The keyword map should stay focused on buyer language, not expand into broad productivity SEO.

## Smallest fixes needed before live demos

1. Keep the founder-OS thesis as the first sentence in repo-first summaries.
2. Keep the keyword and competitor tasks tightly founder-facing.
3. Make the blog brief template more opinionated about founder control, prioritization, and approvals.
4. Treat Northstar-on-Northstar as the release gate for future calibration passes.
