# Northstar Dogfood Findings

This note captures what Northstar generated when Northstar was used as its own pilot workspace.

## Before fix

Northstar previously misread itself as a GitHub-first agentic QA product. The generated board leaned into:

- QA language
- overnight audits
- repo credibility
- generic B2B content tasks

That was the wrong product thesis for Northstar.

## After fix

Northstar now reads itself as a Kanban-first operating system for non-technical founders.

The generated output now centers on:

- founder-facing positioning
- a board-led growth command center
- keyword mapping around founder operating systems and board-led execution
- a blog brief on why non-technical founders need a board, not another chat thread
- competitor framing around founder tools, approvals, and board-first execution

## What Northstar generated for itself

### Company understanding
- Company summary: Northstar appears to be a Kanban-first operating system for non-technical founders. The core promise is that a founder can connect a website, generate a prioritized growth backlog, and manage execution and approvals from one board instead of juggling disconnected marketing work.
- ICP: Non-technical founders and lean startup operators who need a visible system to prioritize, supervise, and approve growth work without hiring a full marketing team.
- Opportunities:
  - The product promise is strong, but the first-time story should explain faster why a founder should trust a board-led operating system over a chat-first assistant.
  - The repo shows execution credibility, but Northstar still needs sharper public proof that the board creates prioritized, founder-usable growth work rather than generic task lists.
  - The best growth angle is likely dogfooding and operator education, but that point of view needs clearer landing-page and content framing.

### Generated backlog
- Clarify why Northstar is a founder operating system, not just another chatbot
- Map the search language around founder operating systems, marketing boards, and execution visibility
- Draft a blog brief on why non-technical founders need a board, not another chat thread
- Review how adjacent founder tools frame prioritization, visibility, and approvals
- Create a founder-proof signal set from Northstar's own dogfood results

### Artifact
- Blog brief: `Draft a blog brief on why non-technical founders need a board, not another chat thread`
- Direction: explain the before-state, the operating model, and why visible prioritization beats disconnected AI chats

## What looked strong

- The system now correctly understands Northstar's product thesis.
- The ICP is grounded in non-technical founders and lean startup operators.
- The opportunity list now points at the board-led operating system story instead of QA language.
- The top task is specific and useful to the business.
- The blog brief structure is coherent and aligned with the product's point of view.
- The approval flow still works end to end after the fix.

## What looked weak or generic

- The backlog still needs a final copy polish pass so the founder-OS story feels even crisper in live demos.
- The keyword map and competitor scan are useful, but they still need to stay sharp enough to avoid generic productivity language.
- The company is still being framed through the repo surface, even though the product thesis is now correct.
- The content angle should stay opinionated around founder control, approvals, and growth work rather than drifting into generic task management talk.

## Where it confuses the business model

- The repo is still proof, but the product is now correctly understood as a founder operating system, not a QA product.
- The remaining risk is drift toward generic productivity language if the founder-OS thesis is not kept front and center.
- The content system is right to suggest SEO, but it should stay grounded in the non-technical founder GTM and not drift into generic content marketing.

## Smallest fixes needed

1. Keep the founder-OS thesis at the top of the summary in future passes.
2. Tighten the keyword cluster and competitor scan language so they stay clearly founder-facing.
3. Reduce generic productivity-task phrasing if future outputs start drifting away from the board-led story.
4. Keep the repo as proof, not as the category label.
5. Use Northstar-on-Northstar as the first demo gate for future calibration passes.

## Priority order

1. Buyer-facing homepage narrative
2. Blog brief quality for the operating model
3. Founder-OS opportunity framing
4. Better category language for search/content
