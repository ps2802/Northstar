# Northstar Demo Runbook

Use this runbook for the current v1 demo. The goal is to show trust, board-first product shape, and one clean approval moment.

## Primary demo: Northstar on Northstar

### Why this is the primary demo

- It proves Northstar can create useful growth work for itself.
- It shows the board as the main product surface.
- It gives the strongest point-of-view artifact in the current build.

### Demo input

- Website URL: `https://github.com/ps2802/Northstar`

### Demo flow

1. Start on onboarding.
2. Enter the Northstar GitHub URL.
3. Call out the product promise immediately after analysis:
   - Kanban-first operating system for non-technical founders
4. Show the company summary.
5. Show the ICP.
6. Show the opportunities.
7. Open the board and focus on the first two tasks.
8. Open the top task drawer and read the rationale sections:
   - why this exists
   - why this priority
   - business outcome supported
9. Open the generated blog brief.
10. Approve the brief.
11. Show the linked task move to `Done`.

### What to say during the demo

- "Northstar is not trying to be another chat assistant. The board is the product."
- "The system turns a website into visible, prioritized growth work with rationale."
- "The founder can add work, review generated artifacts, and approve outcomes from the same operating surface."

### Expected Northstar output

#### Company summary
- Northstar appears to be a Kanban-first operating system for non-technical founders.

#### ICP
- Non-technical founders and lean startup operators who need a visible system to prioritize, supervise, and approve growth work.

#### Opportunities
- explain why a founder should trust a board-led operating system over a chat-first assistant
- show stronger public proof that the board creates prioritized, founder-usable growth work
- lean into dogfooding and operator education

#### Top backlog
1. Make Northstar's board-first value obvious to a non-technical founder in one screen
2. Draft a blog brief on why non-technical founders need a board, not another chat thread
3. Build a keyword map around founder operating systems and board-led growth execution
4. Create a proof-led founder signal set from Northstar's own dogfood results

### Best artifact moment

The strongest artifact moment is the brief titled:
- `Draft a blog brief on why non-technical founders need a board, not another chat thread`

What makes it work:
- it is opinionated
- it reinforces the board-first thesis
- it gives the founder language they can repeat in demos and conversations

## Secondary demo: Gridlock

### Why Gridlock is the backup case

- It is easy for a live audience to understand quickly.
- It proves Northstar is not trapped in B2B language.
- The output is clear, event-driven, and visually easy to explain.

### Demo input

- Website URL: `https://joingridlock.com/`

### What to show

1. Company summary
2. ICP
3. Event-driven opportunities
4. Top backlog ordering

### Expected Gridlock output

#### Company summary
- Gridlock appears to be a Formula 1 prediction game built for fans who care about qualifying, grid outcomes, and outperforming consensus.

#### ICP
- Formula 1 fans who follow qualifying closely and want a more skill-driven prediction game than casual pick-em products.

#### Opportunities
- explain the season loop and retention mechanics more clearly
- lean into qualifying and race-weekend content moments
- improve discoverability beyond the homepage

#### Top backlog
1. Sharpen the homepage story for serious F1 fans joining Gridlock
2. Create an X post set for qualifying-day engagement
3. Plan a race-weekend content cluster for Gridlock
4. Draft a blog brief on why F1 predictions should reward skill over consensus

## Demo guardrails

- Do not pitch it as autonomous publishing.
- Do not pitch it as a team collaboration suite.
- Do not over-explain internals.
- Keep returning to the same point:
  - Northstar turns messy growth work into a visible operating system.

## If something feels weak live

- stay on Northstar longer and emphasize rationale + approval flow
- use Gridlock next because it is the easiest audience reset
- avoid over-indexing on the competitor-scan task because it is less impressive than the homepage and brief tasks
